Research

Ongoing Research

I am currently conducting research at the School of Energy Science and Engineering, Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur, under the mentorship of Dr. Mayur Prakash Bonkile. My present work focuses on physics-based battery modelling, aimed at developing high-fidelity numerical frameworks to predict electrochemical and thermal behaviour of advanced battery systems.

My co-supervisor is Prof. Jeevanjyoti Chakrabarty from the Department of Mechanical Engineering, IIT Kharagpur. This research involves multiphysics modelling, simulation-driven design, and computational approaches for next-generation energy storage technologies.

Doctoral Research

I completed my doctoral research at the Heat Transfer and Thermal Power Laboratory, Department of Mechanical Engineering, Indian Institute of Technology Madras, under the supervision of Prof. C. Balaji.

My doctoral work focused on electronic cooling and battery thermal management, addressing high heat flux challenges in modern electronic and energy systems. The research was primarily experimental, complemented by targeted numerical modelling for validation, performance evaluation, and design optimisation.

The work contributed to improved understanding of cyclic heat load behaviour, thermal mitigation strategies, and the development of hybrid cooling architectures for electronics and battery systems.
